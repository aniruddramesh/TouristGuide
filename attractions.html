Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is one of the most famous tourist destinations in India. It is a breathtaking example of Indo-Saracenic architecture, blending Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic styles. The palace is illuminated with over 100,000 bulbs during special occasions, creating a magical spectacle.

Visitors can explore the ornate interiors, including the Durbar Hall, the Kalyana Mantapa, and the Portrait Gallery. Don't miss the Sound and Light show that narrates the palace's history.

Chamundi Hills

Chamundi Hills, located about 13 km from Mysore city, is a popular pilgrimage and tourist site. The hills are named after Goddess Chamundeshwari, the patron deity of the Mysore Maharajas.

Attractions on Chamundi Hills include:

  • Chamundeshwari Temple: A beautiful 12th-century temple at the hilltop
  • Nandi Statue: A massive 16 ft tall statue of Nandi, Lord Shiva's mount
  • Panoramic Views: Breathtaking views of Mysore city from various viewpoints

Brindavan Gardens

The Brindavan Gardens, adjoining the Krishna Raja Sagara (KRS) Dam, is a beautifully landscaped garden known for its symmetric design and musical fountain. Spread over 60 acres, it's a perfect spot for evening relaxation and picnics.

Key features include:

  • Musical Fountain Show: A spectacular display of water, lights, and music
  • Boating: Enjoy a serene boat ride on the garden's calm waters
  • Topiary Work: Admire the artistic plant sculptures throughout the garden

Mysore Zoo

The Mysore Zoo, officially known as Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens, is one of the oldest and most popular zoos in India. Established in 1892, it spans over 157 acres and is home to a wide variety of species.

Highlights of the zoo include:

  • Diverse Wildlife: See animals like white tigers, African elephants, gorillas, and many more
  • Karanji Lake: A large lake within the zoo complex, perfect for bird watching
  • Conservation Programs: Learn about the zoo's efforts in breeding endangered species
